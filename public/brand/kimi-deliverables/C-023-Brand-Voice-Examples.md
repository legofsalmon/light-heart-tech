# Brand Voice Guide: Extended Examples

**Document Type:** Operational reference for content creators  
**Audience:** RAD team, social media managers, content writers, external agencies  
**Status:** Active  
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## Introduction

This document provides practical examples of Lightheart's brand voice across 10 common communication scenarios. Each scenario includes:
- **DO:** An example that embodies our voice (Confident/Technical/Irish/Warm/Institutional)
- **DON'T:** An example that falls into common traps (Arrogant/Inaccessible/Parochial/Twee/Corporate)
- **WHY:** Brief explanation of what works and what doesn't

Reference the **5 Tensions** from strategy:
1. **Confident, not Arrogant** — We know our value without overstating it
2. **Technical, not Inaccessible** — We name our equipment precisely but explain why it matters
3. **Irish, not Parochial** — Local context, global ambition
4. **Warm, not Twee** — Human without being cutesy
5. **Institutional, not Corporate** — Serious without being soulless

## 1. INSTAGRAM CAPTION — Exhibition Announcement

### DO ✓

> **Digital Nature** opens September 18.
>
> We've commissioned seven Irish artists to create new work for our opening. No screens on walls. No headphones. 37 Barco projectors. 87 speakers. One room becomes forest, another becomes shore.
>
> The work will exist only here. After 12 weeks, it comes down, and we start again.
>
> Tickets on sale Monday, 10am. Link in bio.

**Why this works:**
- Specific numbers (37, 87) show technical confidence without bragging
- "No screens on walls" addresses the immersive format without saying "immersive experience"
- Clear timeline creates urgency without hype
- "We start again" reinforces quarterly model

### DON'T ✗

> ✨ We're thrilled to announce our GRAND OPENING! ✨ Step into a world of wonder at Lightheart Immersive Gallery — Dublin's most cutting-edge digital art destination! 🎨
>
> Prepare to be amazed by jaw-dropping visuals and mind-blowing technology! Book now before tickets sell out!
>
> #immersiveart #dublin #digitalart #mustsee #unmissable #cuttingedge

**Why this fails:**
- "Step into" and "immersive experience" are banned phrases
- Multiple exclamation points and emojis = twee
- "Jaw-dropping" / "mind-blowing" = unspecific hype
- "Most cutting-edge" = arrogant claim without proof
- Generic hashtags without local context

## 2. LINKEDIN POST — New Team Hire

### DO ✓

> We're pleased to announce that Sarah Byrne joins Lightheart as Visitor Experience Manager, starting June 1.
>
> Sarah spent six years at IMMA developing accessibility programmes, and she'll bring that expertise to our front-of-house operations. She starts three months before opening, which means she'll help shape how we welcome people from day one.
>
> We're still hiring for technical and front-of-house roles. Details: lightheart.ie/careers

**Why this works:**
- Credentials mentioned (IMMA) establish credibility
- Specific start date shows planning
- "Help shape" = warm invitation to contribute
- Direct link to careers = institutional efficiency
- No over-the-top praise or welcome wagon language

### DON'T ✗

> 🎉 PLEASE JOIN US IN WELCOMING Sarah to the Lightheart family!!! 🎉
>
> We are SO EXCITED to have her on board as she brings her AMAZING energy and PASSION for the arts! Sarah's going to help us deliver WORLD-CLASS experiences to our visitors!
>
> Welcome to the team, Sarah! We can't wait to see what AMAZING things we'll achieve together! 💫
>
> #dreamteam #newhire #excited #passion #family

**Why this fails:**
- "Family" language = corporate twee
- All caps = shouting
- "World-class" = unverifiable claim
- No actual information about what she'll do
- Generic corporate enthusiasm without substance

## 3. EMAIL RESPONSE — Press Enquiry

### DO ✓

> Subject: Re: Press enquiry — Lightheart opening
>
> Hi Ciarán,
>
> Thanks for getting in touch. I'm attaching our press pack, which covers the venue, the September exhibition, and our quarterly programming model.
>
> For interviews with Brendan Dillon (founder) or Sarah Chen (opening artist), let me know your deadline and we can arrange. We're scheduling press previews for the week of September 8.
>
> High-res images are here: [link]. Please credit photographers as listed in the image notes.
>
> Best,
> [Name]
> Press Office, Lightheart
> press@lightheart.ie

**Why this works:**
- Efficient, no fluff
- Specific actions offered (interviews, previews)
- Clear timeline (September 8)
- Practical details (image credits) = professional
- No unnecessary enthusiasm or relationship-building language

### DON'T ✗

> Subject: RE: Press enquiry
>
> Dear Ciarán,
>
> Thank you so much for your interest in Lightheart! We are absolutely delighted that you are considering featuring us in your publication!
>
> It would be our absolute pleasure to assist you in any way possible! Please find attached some materials that may be of interest! We would be honoured to work with you and look forward to building a wonderful relationship!
>
> Warmest regards,
> [Name]
> Senior Media Relations & Communications Executive
> Lightheart Immersive Gallery Experience

**Why this fails:**
- Multiple exclamation points = desperate
- "Delighted" / "honoured" / "wonderful relationship" = corporate over-familiarity
- No specific information offered
- Bloated job title = trying too hard
- No clear next steps or practical details

## 4. FOUNDING CIRCLE REJECTION LETTER — Oversubscribed

### DO ✓

> Subject: Lightheart Founding Circle — Application Outcome
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for applying to the Founding Circle. We've received more applications than available places (200), and we're unable to offer you membership at this time.
>
> We're maintaining a waitlist in case places become available. If you'd like to join it, reply to this email.
>
> General admission tickets go on sale September 1. If you sign up to our newsletter, you'll be first to know when booking opens.
>
> Thanks again for your interest.
>
> [Name]
> membership@lightheart.ie

**Why this works:**
- Clear, honest rejection
- Specific number (200) reinforces scarcity
- Waitlist option = not closing door completely
- Alternative offered (newsletter, general tickets)
- No fake sympathy or over-explaining

### DON'T ✗

> Subject: Your Lightheart Founding Circle Application
>
> Dear Valued Applicant,
>
> Thank you for your interest in becoming a Founding Member of the Lightheart family! After careful consideration of all applications, we regret to inform you that we are unable to offer you a place at this time.
>
> Please don't take this personally — it was a very difficult decision! We received SO many wonderful applications from AMAZING people like yourself!
>
> We'd love to stay connected! Follow us on social media for all the latest updates!
>
> Warmly,
> The Lightheart Team

**Why this fails:**
- "Valued Applicant" = impersonal corporate speak
- "Don't take this personally" = condescending
- "SO many AMAZING people" = twee over-familiarity
- No waitlist option or alternative path offered
- "Follow us on social media" = lazy deflection

## 5. EXHIBITION WALL TEXT — 60 Words

### DO ✓

> **The Shore at Night**
> Cian Ó Broin, 2026
>
> Ó Broin filmed the Dublin Bay shoreline for six months, then trained an AI model on the footage. What you're seeing isn't video — it's the AI's reconstruction of tide, moon, and weather patterns, rendered in real time.
>
> The sound is captured live: 32 microphones placed along the bay. What you hear is happening now.

**Word count:** 56  
**Why this works:**
- Specific process explained (six months, 32 microphones)
- "What you're seeing" = direct address
- Technical detail (AI model, real-time) made accessible
- Dublin Bay = local context
- Present tense = immediacy

### DON'T ✗

> **The Shore at Night**
> Cian Ó Broin
>
> Step into a mesmerising journey where art and technology collide! This groundbreaking immersive experience uses state-of-the-art AI and cutting-edge projection technology to transport you to the shores of Dublin Bay!
>
> Prepare to be amazed as you discover the breathtaking beauty of nature reimagined through digital innovation! An unforgettable experience awaits!

**Word count:** 52  
**Why this fails:**
- "Step into" = banned phrase
- "Collide" / "transport" / "unforgettable" = cliché
- No specific information about the work
- Three exclamation points = desperate enthusiasm
- "State-of-the-art" / "groundbreaking" = unverifiable claims

## 6. ERROR/404 PAGE COPY

### DO ✓

> **Page not found**
>
> That address doesn't exist, or it moved.
>
> [Return to homepage]
> [View current exhibitions]
> [Contact us]

**Why this works:**
- Plain statement of fact
- No forced personality or jokes
- Clear actions offered
- Institutional clarity over cleverness

### DON'T ✗

> **Oops! Looks like you got lost! 🙈**
>
> Don't worry — even the best of us take a wrong turn sometimes! The page you're looking for seems to have wandered off into the digital wilderness!
>
> But hey, while you're here, why not explore our AMAZING exhibitions instead? Click below to discover a world of wonder!
>
> [Take me home!] [See what's on!]

**Why this fails:**
- "Oops" / "got lost" = twee personification
- Monkey emoji = infantilising
- "Digital wilderness" = trying too hard to be clever
- "AMAZING" / "world of wonder" = empty hype
- Multiple exclamation points = anxious energy

## 7. TICKET CONFIRMATION EMAIL

### DO ✓

> Subject: Your Lightheart booking — 14 October 2026, 2:00pm
>
> **Booking confirmed**
>
> Date: Wednesday 14 October 2026
> Time: 2:00pm
> Exhibition: Digital Nature
> Tickets: 2 adults
> Booking reference: LH-20261014-2847
>
> Arrive up to 15 minutes early. Entry closes 30 minutes after your slot.
>
> Location: 21-25 Arran Quay, Dublin 7. Closest Luas: Four Courts.
>
> Questions? Reply to this email.
>
> [Add to calendar] [View/cancel booking]

**Why this works:**
- All essential information above fold
- Specific timing instructions (15 min early, 30 min cutoff)
- Practical transport info
- Clear contact method
- No unnecessary branding or enthusiasm

### DON'T ✗

> Subject: 🎉 Your AMAZING Lightheart booking is CONFIRMED! 🎉
>
> Hi there!
>
> We are THRILLED to confirm your booking for Digital Nature! Get ready for an UNFORGETTABLE experience!
>
> **Your Details:**
> ✨ Date: 14 Oct 2026
> ✨ Time: 2pm
> ✨ Tickets: 2
>
> We can't WAIT to welcome you! Please arrive 15 minutes early! Don't be late!
>
> See you soon!
> The Lightheart Team 💛

**Why this fails:**
- Subject line = spam filter bait
- "UNFORGETTABLE experience" = presuming to tell them how they'll feel
- Emoji bullets = twee
- "We can't WAIT" = false enthusiasm
- Missing practical information (address, transport)
- No booking reference for customer service

## 8. RESPONSE TO NEGATIVE REVIEW

### DO ✓

> Thank you for your feedback.
>
> You're right that the space can feel crowded during peak times. We've limited tickets per session and added additional midweek slots to address this.
>
> If you'd like to discuss your visit, email me directly: [name]@lightheart.ie.
>
> [Name]
> Visitor Experience Manager

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges specific complaint without defensiveness
- Concrete action taken (limited tickets, added slots)
- Direct contact offered = accountability
- No apology for the art/exhibition itself
- Professional signature establishes authority

### DON'T ✗

> Oh no! We're SO SORRY you didn't enjoy your visit! 😢
>
> We work SO HARD to make every experience PERFECT for our AMAZING visitors! Sometimes things don't go to plan, and we feel TERRIBLE about that!
>
> We'd LOVE to make it up to you! Please DM us and we'll send you FREE tickets to come back! We PROMISE it'll be better next time!
>
> Thanks for being part of the Lightheart family! 💛

**Why this fails:**
- Excessive apology = undermines institutional confidence
- Multiple exclamation points = panic
- "Perfect" / "amazing" = hyperbolic
- Free tickets = bribery, not accountability
- "Family" language = twee
- "Promise it'll be better" = setting up future disappointment

>
> Would you be open to a 20-minute call to discuss? I'm available [specific times] or can work around your schedule.
>
> [Name]
> partnerships@lightheart.ie
> [LinkedIn profile]

**Why this works:**
- Clear value proposition in opening
- Specific reason for outreach = not generic
- Concrete numbers (€10,000) = transparency
- Specific availability = respect for their time
- Professional contact details

### DON'T ✗

> Subject: Exciting partnership opportunity!
>
> Dear Sir/Madam,
>
> I hope this email finds you well!
>
> I am writing to explore potential synergies between our organisations! Lightheart is Dublin's premier immersive art destination, and we believe a collaboration could be mutually beneficial!
>
> We offer a range of partnership packages with amazing benefits! I'd love to hop on a quick call to tell you more!
>
> Looking forward to hearing from you!
>
> Best,
> [Name]
> Business Development Executive

**Why this fails:**
- "Hope this finds you well" = generic opener
- "Synergies" / "mutually beneficial" = meaningless corporate speak
- "Premier" = unverifiable claim
- No specific reason for outreach
- "Hop on a quick call" = casual for professional context
- No concrete information about partnership

## 10. ARTIST COMMISSION INVITATION

### DO ✓

> Subject: Commission opportunity — Lightheart 2027
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm [Name], Artistic Director at Lightheart. We're a new permanent gallery in Dublin with 37 Barco projectors, L-ISA spatial audio, and a quarterly exhibition model. Each show runs 12 weeks, then we reset the space entirely.
>
> I've followed your work since [specific piece/show]. Your approach to [specific technique/theme] would translate well to our format — particularly your use of [specific detail].
>
> We're commissioning for Q2 2027 (April–June). Budget is €[range], including production, installation, and artist fee. Timeline: concept by July, production August–March, install April.
>
> Interested? I'll send the full brief if so.
>
> [Name]
> artistic@lightheart.ie

**Why this works:**
- Technical specifications = serious venue
- Specific knowledge of their work = genuine interest
- Clear timeline and budget = professional respect
- Specific quarter (Q2 2027) = concrete opportunity
- Simple ask at the end

### DON'T ✗

> Subject: AMAZING opportunity for artists!
>
> Hi there!
>
> We're Lightheart — Dublin's most exciting new art space! We're looking for AMAZING artists to create WORLD-CLASS immersive experiences for our visitors!
>
> If you're passionate about pushing boundaries and creating unforgettable moments, we want to hear from you! This is your chance to be part of something truly SPECIAL!
>
> Send us your portfolio and tell us why YOU should be chosen!
>
> Can't wait to see your AMAZING work!
>
> The Lightheart Team ✨

**Why this fails:**
- "AMAZING opportunity" = desperate marketing
- "World-class" / "unforgettable" / "special" = empty hype
- No technical information about the venue
- No specific artist addressed = mass email
- "Why YOU should be chosen" = reality show framing
- No budget or timeline mentioned
- Exclamation points and sparkle emoji = twee

## QUICK REFERENCE: VOICE CHECKLIST

Before publishing, check your copy against these five tensions:

| Tension | Check | Red Flag |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Confident, not Arrogant** | We state facts about our work | Words like "best," "premier," "world-class" |
| **Technical, not Inaccessible** | Specific equipment/process named | Jargon without explanation OR dumbing down |
| **Irish, not Parochial** | Dublin location referenced | Assuming Ireland is unknown OR overselling "Irishness" |
| **Warm, not Twee** | Human and direct | Emojis, excessive exclamation points, "family" language |
| **Institutional, not Corporate** | Clear and efficient | Buzzwords, over-apologising, forced enthusiasm |

---

## BANNED PHRASES REFERENCE

Never use in any context:
- "Immersive experience" (say "exhibition" or "installation")
- "Step into" (say "enter," "visit," or omit)
- "Cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" (name the technology instead)
- "World-class" (prove it with details)
- "Unforgettable" / "jaw-dropping" / "breathtaking" (let them decide)
- "Family" to describe staff or customers
- "We can't wait" / "We're thrilled" / "We're excited" (assume interest, don't beg)
