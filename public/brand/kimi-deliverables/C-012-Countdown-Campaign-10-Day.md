## DAY 10 — "We Built Something"

### Instagram
10 days.

Three years ago, this was an idea. Today, it's 37 projectors, 87 speakers, 280 square metres on Dublin's quays.

We didn't adapt a warehouse. We built infrastructure specifically for immersive art. Every projector angle calculated. Every speaker positioned for spatial audio. Every surface treated for projection.

Next week, the first visitors walk through these doors.

10 days until Lightheart opens.

### LinkedIn
10 days until Ireland's first purpose-built immersive gallery opens to the public.

I want to tell you what "purpose-built" means.

It means €4-4.5 million invested before the first ticket is sold. It means 37 projectors permanently installed at precise angles, not rented for exhibitions. It means L-ISA spatial audio infrastructure designed into the architecture, not retrofitted afterwards.

It means we made a bet: that immersive art deserves permanent infrastructure, not temporary accommodation. That Dublin deserves a space where artists can create work that couldn't exist elsewhere.

For three years, this was drawings and specifications. Next week, it's real.

10 days.

## DAY 7 — The Artist (Pauric Freeman)

### Instagram
Day 7: The artist.

Pauric Freeman. Dublin-born, Berlin-based. Creates environments where digital processes behave like nature.

"Digital Nature" isn't projection. It's presence. The space responds to you. Breathes with the room. Exists only when you're inside it.

Pauric has been preparing this for Lightheart specifically. Not a touring show. Not an adaptation. Something built for these walls, these projectors, these sensors.

7 days until you stand inside it.

### LinkedIn
7 days until our inaugural exhibition opens.

I want to introduce you to Pauric Freeman — the artist who will be the first to create work specifically for Lightheart's infrastructure.

Pauric is Dublin-born, Berlin-based. His practice sits at the intersection of real-time graphics and organic behaviour. He doesn't project nature onto walls. He creates systems that behave like nature — responsive, unpredictable, alive only when engaged.

"Digital Nature" is not a video installation. It's an environment that processes presence in real-time. The 44 depth-sensing cameras we installed aren't for surveillance. They're sensory organs. They allow the space to perceive and respond.

This is what happens when you give an artist infrastructure designed specifically for their practice.

7 days.
