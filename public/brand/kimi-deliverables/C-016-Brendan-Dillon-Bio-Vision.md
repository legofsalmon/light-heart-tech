# Brendan Dillon — Founder Biography & Vision

---

## 1. Founder Biography (250 words)

**For:** About page, press kit, institutional communications  
**Length:** Approximately 250 words  
**Tone:** Professional, personal, grounded

---

Brendan Dillon is the founder of Lightheart Immersive Gallery, Ireland's first purpose-built immersive art space, opening in Dublin in September 2026.

A Dublin native, Dillon spent over fifteen years observing the evolution of immersive art internationally — from teamLab in Tokyo to ARTECHOUSE in Washington, from Frameless in London to Meow Wolf in Santa Fe. What struck him was not just the work itself, but the infrastructure that enabled it: permanent, purpose-built spaces where technology and architecture were designed together.

"I kept coming back to Dublin and looking around," Dillon recalls. "We have galleries that hang art on walls. We have theatres that stage performances. What we don't have is a space where you stand inside the art. Where the walls, floor, and ceiling become a single canvas. Where technology serves wonder rather than distraction."

In 2023, Dillon decided to build it himself.

Lightheart represents a €4-4.5 million investment in permanent infrastructure — 37 projectors, 87 speakers, 280 square metres designed specifically for immersive art. The quarterly exhibition model means the space transforms completely every twelve weeks, creating both anticipation and return engagement.

Dillon's vision extends beyond the exhibitions themselves. He sees Lightheart as community infrastructure — a space where artists develop work that couldn't exist elsewhere, where audiences experience collective wonder, and where Dublin's cultural landscape expands to match the ambition of its artists.

"I got tired of waiting for someone else to build this," Dillon says. "So I decided to build it."

## 2. Founder Vision Statement (400 words)

**For:** About page, membership materials, donor communications  
**Length:** Approximately 400 words  
**Tone:** First-person, vulnerable, ambitious, grounded

---

I could have done this differently.

When I started planning Lightheart, everyone told me to rent a warehouse. Install projectors for six weeks. Call it "immersive" and move on. That's the model. Pop-ups in shopping centres. Temporary installations in borrowed spaces. Low risk, quick return.

I thought about it. €4-4.5 million is a terrifying amount to invest in something that doesn't exist yet. The safer route was tempting.

But immersive art deserves permanent infrastructure. Artists working with real-time systems, spatial audio, responsive environments — they need spaces designed for their practice. Not adapted warehouses where they're fighting the architecture. Not temporary rigs where they can't refine their work. Spaces where projectors are calibrated to the centimetre, acoustic treatment is built in, and the technical team knows every quirk.

Dublin deserves that. This city has produced extraordinary artists for generations, and we've watched too many leave because the spaces they needed didn't exist here. I got tired of waiting for someone else to fix it.

So I committed to the hard version. The permanent one. Where architecture, technology, and operations are designed specifically for this form.

The quarterly model matters to me. Every twelve weeks, Lightheart transforms completely. New artist. New vision. New world. This isn't novelty for novelty's sake — it's about giving each exhibition time to find its audience, evolve through its run, be properly experienced. It creates rhythm. Anticipation. I want people to return because they know something extraordinary is always coming.

When you leave Lightheart, I want you to feel like you've been somewhere else. Not like you watched something — like you were inside something that changed how you feel about possibility. I want strangers to stand together in silence because they're experiencing something none of them have words for. I want you to forget your phone for fifty minutes because the world around you became too interesting to look away from.

My ten-year vision isn't revenue or expansion. It's community infrastructure. Lightheart as space where relationships form — between artists and audiences, strangers who experience wonder together, Dublin and the ambitious cultural city it can become. Where artists develop work they couldn't make anywhere else. Where audiences discover they need this.

I don't know if this will work. That's the truth. But not building it — accepting Dublin doesn't get to have this — was worse.

So we're building it.

September. I'll see you inside.

— Brendan Dillon  
Founder, Lightheart

**Biography:** Use complete or excerpted for press materials, grant applications, speaker introductions, and institutional communications. Suitable for third-person contexts.

**Vision Statement:** Use in full for About page "Founder's Letter" section, membership programme materials, and donor communications. The first-person voice creates personal connection and demonstrates commitment. Do not edit for length without preserving the arc: the temptation of the easy path, the decision to commit, the vision for what this becomes.

**Photo Pairing:** Both pieces work best with candid photography of Brendan in the space — not corporate headshots. Images that show him looking at construction, talking with team members, or experiencing the space himself.
