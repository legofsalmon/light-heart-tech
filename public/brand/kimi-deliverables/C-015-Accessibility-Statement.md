## Our Commitment

Lightheart is built on the belief that art should be available to everyone. This isn't a compliance exercise. It's a core value.

Our fourth curatorial principle — **Pluralism** — means platforming voices and experiences that are often excluded. That applies to our visitors as much as our artists. A space that surrounds you with art is only successful if everyone who wants to can enter, experience, and return.

We designed Lightheart with accessibility in mind from the beginning. The step-free entrance isn't an adaptation. The wheelchair spaces aren't afterthoughts. The sensory guides aren't produced last-minute. These were part of the architectural brief, the technical specification, and the operational planning.

We're not perfect. We know that. But we are committed to listening, learning, and improving. If you encounter a barrier we haven't addressed, we want to know. We'll fix what we can and be honest about what we can't.

## Physical Access

### Getting Here

Lightheart has step-free access from the street to all public areas. No ramps added later. No alternative entrances. One level path from the pavement to the centre of the gallery.

**Parking:** We have two accessible parking spaces directly outside the entrance. These cannot be booked in advance, but if you need one, arrive early or contact us and we'll do our best to reserve it. The surrounding streets have additional accessible parking bays.

**Public Transport:** The venue is served by [bus routes TBD] and is a [distance TBD] walk from [LUAS stop TBD]. The path from the LUAS stop is step-free and reasonably level, though the quayside paving can be uneven in places.

### Inside the Galleries

Both Room 1 and Room 2 are fully wheelchair accessible. The floor surface is smooth and even throughout — suitable for wheelchairs, mobility scooters, and walking aids.

**Wheelchair Spaces:**
- Room 1: 6 designated wheelchair spaces with clear sightlines to all projection surfaces
- Room 2: 4 designated wheelchair spaces

These spaces are held until 10 minutes before each session begins, then released to the general queue. If you need a wheelchair space, we recommend booking in advance.

**Seating:** Portable seating is available in both galleries. If you need to sit during the 50-minute experience, just ask a staff member when you arrive.

**Accessible Toilets:** Fully accessible toilets are located between the two galleries. They include:
- Step-free access
- Grab rails
- Emergency alarm
- Full turning circle for wheelchairs
- Height-adjusted fixtures

**Assistance Dogs:** Registered assistance dogs are welcome throughout Lightheart. If your assistance dog needs water or a quiet space, just ask any staff member.

**Induction Loops:** Hearing loops are installed at the reception desk and in both galleries. If you use a hearing aid with a T-setting, switch to the T position.

## Sensory Considerations

Lightheart exhibitions are immersive by design. They involve:
- **Loud sound:** The L-ISA spatial audio system can reach significant volume levels
- **Darkness:** Sessions include periods of low light and darkness
- **Rapid visual change:** Projected imagery changes quickly; some sequences could affect people with photosensitive epilepsy
- **Spatial disorientation:** The immersive environment can affect balance and spatial awareness

We don't apologise for these elements — they're integral to the art. But we do provide alternatives and supports so you can choose how to engage.

### Relaxed Sessions

Every exhibition includes at least one relaxed session per week.

**What changes:**
- Volume reduced by approximately 30%
- Lighting levels raised slightly (no total darkness)
- No sudden sound or visual transitions
- Freedom to move, make noise, or leave and re-enter
- Smaller group size (maximum 20 people)

**When:** [Day TBD] at [time TBD] during each exhibition run

**Booking:** Relaxed sessions are listed on our booking page. If they're not visible, the exhibition may not be suitable for modification — contact us and we'll advise.

### Audio Description

Audio description is available for all Lightheart exhibitions.

**What you get:**
- Pre-visit descriptive guide (emailed or mailed in advance)
- Live audio description track during the session (delivered via personal receiver)
- Touch tour before the session (optional, 15 minutes, includes tactile models of the space and key visual elements)

**Booking:** Request audio description when booking your ticket. We need at least 48 hours notice to prepare the live description.

### BSL-Interpreted Events

Each exhibition includes at least one BSL-interpreted session.

**What happens:**
- A BSL interpreter is present throughout the 50-minute experience
- Pre-session introduction in BSL explaining what to expect
- Post-session Q&A in BSL with our education team

**When:** [Day TBD] at [time TBD] during each exhibition run

**Booking:** Listed on our booking page. If you need BSL interpretation for a different session, contact us and we'll arrange it where possible.

### Sensory Guides

Before each exhibition opens, we publish a detailed sensory guide.

**What's in it:**
- Detailed description of the visual and audio content
- Sound level information (in decibels, where possible)
- Lighting level information
- Warning about any strobe effects, flashing, or rapid visual changes
- Duration of quiet and loud sections
- Description of any interactive elements
- Photos of the space with and without projections

**How to get it:** Published on our website 2 weeks before each exhibition opens. Available in large print, email, or post on request.

### Quiet Room

A quiet room is available throughout your visit.

**Location:** Between the two galleries, clearly signposted  
**Features:** Soft lighting, comfortable seating, no audio or visual stimuli  
**Use:** Step out at any point during the exhibition if you need a break. You can rejoin your session or wait there until it ends.

If you know in advance that you might need extended quiet time, let us know when you book. We can extend your session or arrange a private viewing if that would help.

## Digital Access

### Website

Our website aims to meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards. This means:
- Text can be resized up to 200% without breaking layout
- Colour contrast meets minimum ratios for readability
- All images have alt text
- All video content has captions
- Navigation works with keyboard-only use
- Forms are properly labelled for screen readers

We're not there yet on everything. We're working through an accessibility audit and fixing issues as we find them. If you encounter a barrier on our website, email accessibility@lightheart.ie and we'll prioritise fixing it.

### Video Content

All video content on our website and social media includes:
- Captions (subtitles)
- Transcripts available on request
- Audio description for key visual-only content

If you find a video without captions, that's an oversight — let us know and we'll fix it within 48 hours.

### Booking System

Our online booking system works with screen readers and keyboard navigation. If you prefer to book by phone, call [phone number TBD] and we'll process your booking manually.

If you need to reserve an accessibility feature (wheelchair space, audio description, etc.), the booking system has a dedicated accessibility section. If you can't find what you need, email accessibility@lightheart.ie and we'll handle it directly.

## Ticketing

### Carer/Companion Tickets

If you need a carer or companion to visit with you, their ticket is free.

**How it works:**
- Book your own ticket through the normal process
- Email accessibility@lightheart.ie with your booking reference and a brief explanation of your access needs
- We add a free companion ticket to your booking
- No documentation required — we trust you

### Wheelchair User + Companion

If you use a wheelchair and want to bring a companion, both tickets are free for the companion.

**How it works:**
- Book your wheelchair space through the booking system (your ticket is charged at standard rate)
- Email accessibility@lightheart.ie to add a free companion ticket

### Relaxed Sessions Pricing

Relaxed sessions are priced the same as standard sessions. We don't charge extra for accessibility, and we don't discount accessible options. The price is the price.

### Founding Circle Membership

Accessibility accommodations apply equally to Founding Circle members. If you're a founding member and need adjusted access to member events, contact accessibility@lightheart.ie and we'll arrange what you need.

## Feedback and Improvement

### Tell Us What Works

If something about your visit was accessible and worked well, we want to know. Positive feedback helps us understand what to keep doing.

Email: accessibility@lightheart.ie  
Subject: "Accessibility Feedback — Positive"

### Tell Us What Doesn't

If you encountered a barrier, something didn't work, or we failed to meet your needs, we need to know that too. We won't be defensive. We'll listen, apologise if we got it wrong, and work out how to fix it.

Email: accessibility@lightheart.ie  
Subject: "Accessibility Feedback — Issue"

**What happens next:**
- We acknowledge your email within 24 hours
- We investigate what happened within 7 days
- We respond with what we found and what we're doing about it
- If it's something we can fix quickly, we fix it
- If it's a bigger issue, we tell you our plan and timeline

### Accessibility Forum

We're establishing an Access Forum — a group of disabled visitors and accessibility advisors who meet quarterly to review our provision and suggest improvements.

If you'd like to be involved, email accessibility@lightheart.ie with subject "Access Forum."

## What We Can't Do (Yet)

We're honest about limitations. There are some things we cannot currently provide:

- **Full visual accessibility:** Our exhibitions are primarily visual experiences. While we provide audio description and touch tours, the core experience relies on sight. We're exploring non-visual alternatives (haptic feedback, spatial audio narratives) but these are not yet available.
- **Complete sound elimination:** Even our relaxed sessions involve significant sound. We cannot currently offer a fully silent experience.
- **Guide dog relief area:** We don't have a dedicated outdoor relief area on site. The nearest suitable area is [location TBD, distance TBD].
- **Changing Places toilet:** Our accessible toilet meets standard accessibility requirements but is not a full Changing Places facility with hoist and changing bench.

If any of these limitations affect you, please contact us. We may be able to suggest alternatives or workarounds. And knowing what people need helps us prioritise future improvements.
