# Lightheart Cafe — Brand & Menu Copy

---

## Cafe Positioning Statement

Lightheart Cafe is a pause, not a pit stop. Before your session, it's a moment to settle in. After, it's where the experience lingers while you find your feet again. We don't do museum cafeteria coffee or pre-wrapped sandwiches. Instead, we serve properly brewed coffee from Irish roasters, loose-leaf tea, and simple seasonal food made by Dublin bakeries and kitchens we know by name. The space is calm, unfussy, and designed for conversation — with companions or with the work you've just experienced. Quality without performance. That's the standard.

## Lightheart-Adjacent Menu Items

Creative names that feel connected to the gallery without trying too hard:

1. **The Projectionist** — Double espresso with a side of sparkling water. For the full focus.

2. **Room Tone** — Our house filter coffee, black. The baseline before the experience begins.

3. **Interval** — A short glass of something cold — sparkling water, local lemonade, or a single espresso over ice. The pause between.

4. **Afterimage** — Decaf espresso with oat milk. The lingering impression without the caffeine.

5. **The Founding Circle** — A pot of loose-leaf tea for the table. For those who were here before the doors opened.

## Cafe Atmosphere Description (80 words)

Lightheart Cafe occupies the ground floor between the street and the galleries — a transitional space designed for settling in or winding down. Natural light, warm wood, and comfortable seating create an atmosphere that's calm without being hushed. It's a place for conversation, for reading, for sitting with what you've just experienced. Quality coffee from Irish roasters, seasonal food from Dublin suppliers, and no pressure to rush. A proper cafe that happens to be in a gallery, not a gallery cafe that serves coffee.

---

## Supplier Ethos Statement

We buy from people we can visit. Irish roasters who know their farmers. Dublin bakers who start before dawn. Small suppliers who care about what they make and how they make it. Seasonal means we take what's good now, not what's been shipped from somewhere else. Sustainable means we choose suppliers who share our standards — on waste, on sourcing, on treatment of people — and we check, not just trust the label. No greenwashing, no certifications we haven't verified, no claims we can't back up. Local where possible. Better where necessary. Honest always.

## Tone Guidelines

✅ **We sound like:**  
- A recommendation from a friend who knows good coffee  
- A Dublin cafe that happens to be in a gallery  
- People who care about ingredients without making it your problem  
- Unfussy, warm, competent

❌ **We never sound like:**  
- A museum cafeteria  
- A chain with a script  
- Pretentious about food or coffee  
- Greenwashing (vague "sustainable" claims without specifics)
