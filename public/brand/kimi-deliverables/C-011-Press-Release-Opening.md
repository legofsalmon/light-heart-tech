# PRESS RELEASE

## Lightheart Immersive Gallery Opens in Dublin

**Ireland's first purpose-built immersive gallery launches September 2026 with €4.5M infrastructure and quarterly artist commissions**

---

**Dublin, June 2026** — Lightheart Immersive Gallery, Ireland's first purpose-built immersive art space, will open its doors in September 2026 on Dublin's quays. The 280-square-metre venue represents a €4-4.5 million investment in permanent infrastructure including 37 projectors, 87 speakers, and real-time interactive systems. Founded by Brendan Dillon and developed in technical partnership with IDIRNET, Lightheart will present quarterly exhibitions of commissioned immersive art, with the inaugural show "Digital Nature" by Berlin-based Irish artist Pauric Freeman opening 12 September 2026.

---

## The Space

Lightheart is not an adapted warehouse or temporary installation. It is purpose-built infrastructure for immersive art — conceived, funded, and constructed specifically for this form.

The venue occupies 280 square metres on Dublin's quays, adjacent to the Guinness Storehouse and within walking distance of the city centre. The space comprises two galleries: Room 1 (120 sq. m.) features 24 projectors and 48 speakers for large-scale collective experiences; Room 2 (intimate scale) contains 13 projectors and 39 speakers for more focused encounters.

Technical infrastructure includes 37 Barco 4K projectors, an L-Acoustics L-ISA spatial audio system with 87 speakers, 44 depth-sensing cameras, and five Pixera media servers for real-time rendering. The €4-4.5 million investment reflects a commitment to permanent, world-class infrastructure rather than temporary or adapted solutions.

The building's architecture was designed in collaboration with the technical team to optimise projection angles, acoustic performance, and visitor flow. Climate control, power distribution, and network architecture were all specified for the demands of immersive art presentation.

---

## The Programme

Lightheart operates on a quarterly exhibition model, with four major exhibitions per year, each running for 12 weeks. This rhythm allows each exhibition to find its audience, evolve through its run, and receive proper documentation before the next installation.

The gallery commissions work specifically for its infrastructure rather than adapting existing exhibitions. Artists collaborate with IDIRNET's technical team from concept through installation, creating experiences that engage Lightheart's specific affordances: precise projection mapping, spatial audio, real-time interactivity, and collective presence design.

The inaugural exhibition, "Digital Nature" by Pauric Freeman, opens 12 September 2026. Freeman, a Dublin-born artist based in Berlin, creates responsive environments where digital processes mimic organic behaviour. The exhibition will use Lightheart's full technical capacity, including real-time sensor integration that allows the environment to respond to collective visitor presence.

Lightheart's curatorial approach is guided by four principles: embodied participation (visitors inside the work, not in front of it), purpose-built specificity (art designed for this exact infrastructure), technogenetic integration (technology as co-author rather than tool), and negentropic practice (duration and depth over fragmentation).

## The Team

**Brendan Dillon — Founder**  
Brendan Dillon conceived Lightheart after fifteen years observing immersive art spaces abroad and identifying the absence of comparable infrastructure in Ireland. He has led the project from initial concept through funding, construction, and launch, assembling a team of creative and technical specialists to realise the vision.

**Kev Freeney — Creative Director, IDIRNET**  
Kev Freeney is Creative Director of IDIRNET, the technical studio that designed Lightheart's infrastructure. With fifteen years' experience in immersive and interactive systems, Freeney led the technical specification and continues as creative partner, collaborating with artists to realise work specifically for Lightheart's capabilities.

**Krisjanis Berzins — Technical Director, IDIRNET**  
Krisjanis Berzins is Technical Director of IDIRNET, responsible for the engineering and implementation of Lightheart's systems. His background in real-time graphics, spatial audio, and sensor integration underpins the venue's technical capacity.

## About Lightheart

Lightheart is Ireland's first purpose-built immersive gallery. Located on Dublin's quays, the 280-square-metre venue combines 37 projectors, 87 speakers, and real-time interactive systems to create exhibitions visitors stand inside rather than observe from a distance. Founded by Brendan Dillon and developed in technical partnership with IDIRNET, Lightheart commissions ambitious works from international artists and presents them in a meticulously designed environment. The gallery operates on a quarterly exhibition model, with each show running for 12 weeks. Lightheart opens to the public in September 2026. For more information, visit lightheart.ie.

---

## About IDIRNET

IDIRNET is a creative technology studio specialising in immersive and interactive systems. Led by Creative Director Kev Freeney and Technical Director Krisjanis Berzins, IDIRNET designed Lightheart's technical infrastructure and continues as the gallery's creative partner, collaborating with artists from concept through installation. The studio brings together expertise in real-time graphics, spatial audio, projection mapping, and sensor integration to enable experiences that engage the full human sensorium.
