Immersive Art in Dublin: Lightheart Gallery

**Ireland's First Purpose-Built Immersive Gallery**

When it comes to immersive art in Dublin, Lightheart represents something genuinely new. This is not a temporary exhibition in an adapted warehouse. It is Ireland's first gallery built specifically for immersive art — from the architecture up.

**What Immersive Art Means at Lightheart**

Immersive art uses technology to create environments you inhabit rather than observe. At Lightheart, this means:

- **360-degree projection:** Artwork that surrounds you on walls, floors, and ceiling
- **Spatial audio:** Sound that moves through three-dimensional space, not just from speakers
- **Real-time responsiveness:** Environments that detect and respond to your presence
- **Collective experience:** Shared moments of wonder with others in the space

**What Lightheart Is Not**

Lightheart differs from other immersive art experiences you may have encountered:

- **Not a selfie museum:** The work resists photography. The experience demands presence, not performance.
- **Not a touring show:** Each exhibition is commissioned specifically for Lightheart's permanent infrastructure. The artists design for these specific 37 projectors and 87 speakers.
- **Not VR:** You remain physically present in a real space. No headsets, no isolation.

**The Quarterly Difference**

Most immersive art Dublin experiences appear temporarily and disappear. Lightheart's quarterly programming means every twelve weeks, the space transforms completely with new commissioned work.

This creates both urgency and return. Each exhibition runs for twelve weeks only, then it's replaced. Visitors who discover Lightheart return to experience what's next.

**The Infrastructure**

Permanent immersive art requires permanent infrastructure:
- 37 Barco 4K projectors at precise angles
- 87 L-Acoustics speakers in L-ISA spatial audio configuration
- 44 depth-sensing cameras for responsive environments
- 280 square metres designed for immersion

**Visiting**

Lightheart is located on Dublin's quays, adjacent to the Guinness Storehouse. Sessions are 50 minutes and should be booked in advance.

If you're seeking immersive art in Dublin that offers genuine artistic ambition and permanent infrastructure, Lightheart is the destination.
