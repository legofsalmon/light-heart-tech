# Education Programme: Schools & Groups

**For:** Primary, secondary, and third-level institutions  
**Location:** Lightheart Immersive Gallery, Dublin  
**Duration:** Approximately 2 hours  
**Booking:** education@lightheart.ie  
**Advance notice:** 4 weeks minimum  
**Status:** Accepting bookings from October 2026

---

## Programme Overview

A school or group visit to Lightheart comprises four components over approximately two hours:

| Component | Duration | Activity |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Arrival & Orientation** | 15 minutes | Meet education facilitator, safety briefing, group division |
| **Guided Gallery Experience** | 50 minutes | Facilitated tour of current exhibition with age-appropriate framing |
| **Workshop** | 40 minutes | Hands-on activity selected from workshop menu |
| **Reflection & Departure** | 15 minutes | Group discussion, Q&A, resource distribution |

**Total Duration:** 2 hours  
**Group Size:** Maximum 30 students per session  
**Adult Ratio:** Minimum 1 adult per 10 students; 1 teacher/educator per group attends free

## Age-Appropriate Programmes

### Primary Level (Ages 8–12)

**Programme Title:** "Sense and Wonder"

**Learning Focus:**  
Sensory exploration, observation skills, introduction to digital art as creative medium, collaborative experience.

**Approach:**  
Students experience the exhibition through guided sensory investigation rather than technical explanation. Facilitators use questioning techniques to develop observation skills: What do you notice? How does it change? What might happen next?

**Key Concepts:**
- Light as artistic material
- Sound as spatial element (sound comes from directions, not just "speakers")
- Digital art responds to presence (the space "notices" us)
- Collective experience (we experience this together)

**Curriculum Links:**
- **Visual Arts:** Responding to visual art; line, shape, colour, texture in digital form
- **Music:** Listening and responding; timbre, dynamics, spatial awareness
- **SPHE:** Group cooperation; emotional response to environment
- **Science:** Light and sound as energy; how eyes and ears work

**Learning Outcomes:**
By the end of the visit, students will be able to:
- Describe how light and sound create environment
- Identify differences between digital and physical art forms
- Express personal response to immersive artwork
- Collaborate in group observation activities

**Language:**  
Facilitators avoid technical terminology. Concepts introduced through experience: "the room knows we're here" rather than "sensor-driven real-time rendering."

---

### Secondary Level (Ages 13–17)

**Programme Title:** "Technology and Creativity"

**Learning Focus:**  
Technical infrastructure, career pathways in creative technology, critical analysis of digital art, ethical considerations of responsive environments.

**Approach:**  
Students receive technical context before experiencing the exhibition, enabling informed observation. Post-experience analysis examines how technical choices serve artistic intent. Career context connects Lightheart's infrastructure to broader creative industries.

**Key Concepts:**
- Real-time rendering vs. pre-recorded content
- Spatial audio design and human perception
- Sensor systems and privacy considerations
- Career pathways: creative technologist, projection designer, spatial audio engineer, immersive artist
- Critical questions: Does technology serve the art? What is gained/lost in digital vs. physical presence?

**Curriculum Links:**
- **Computer Science:** Real-time systems, sensor integration, user interaction design
- **Music:** Spatial audio, acoustic design, technology in performance
- **Visual Arts:** Contemporary art practice, digital media, installation art
- **Physics:** Light (reflection, projection, colour), sound (waves, propagation, interference)
- **SPHE:** Digital wellbeing; presence vs. screen-based experience; collective vs. individual engagement

**Learning Outcomes:**
By the end of the visit, students will be able to:
- Explain basic principles of projection mapping and spatial audio
- Analyse how technical infrastructure enables specific artistic effects
- Identify career roles in immersive technology sector
- Critically evaluate the relationship between technology and artistic experience
- Discuss privacy and consent in responsive environments

**Language:**  
Appropriate technical terminology introduced and explained: projection mapping, spatial audio, real-time rendering, sensor arrays. Facilitators gauge group comprehension and adjust accordingly.

---

### Third Level (Undergraduate/Postgraduate)

**Programme Title:** "Immersive Infrastructure"

**Learning Focus:**  
Technical specification and calibration, curatorial theory for immersive art, spatial audio design principles, professional practice in cultural technology.

**Approach:**  
Specialist-level session combining technical demonstration, theoretical framework, and professional context. Students engage with actual system specifications, calibration data, and curatorial decision-making processes.

**Key Concepts:**
- L-ISA spatial audio: object-based mixing, psychoacoustics, system calibration
- Projection mapping: geometry correction, edge blending, surface treatment
- Real-time systems: latency, synchronisation, render pipelines
- Curatorial theory: embodied participation, technogenetic integration, site-specificity
- Professional practice: artist-technician collaboration, commissioning models, documentation standards

**Curriculum Links:**
- **Music Technology:** Spatial audio systems, immersive sound design, psychoacoustics
- **Computer Science:** Real-time graphics, sensor networks, system architecture
- **Visual Arts/Fine Art:** Installation practice, digital media theory, curatorial studies
- **Architecture/Spatial Design:** Immersive environments, sensory design, human-scale technology
- **Cultural Studies/Politics:** Cultural infrastructure, public space, technology and society

**Learning Outcomes:**
By the end of the visit, students will be able to:
- Analyse technical specifications of immersive systems
- Evaluate curatorial approaches to technology-mediated art
- Understand professional workflows in cultural technology
- Apply theoretical frameworks (site-specificity, technogenetic theory) to observed work
- Assess the role of permanent infrastructure in contemporary art practice

**Language:**  
Full technical and theoretical vocabulary. Facilitators assume familiarity with disciplinary concepts and adapt depth to specific group background (e.g., music technology students vs. fine art students).

## Workshop Menu

School and group visits include one 40-minute workshop selected from the following options. Workshops are adapted for age-appropriate delivery while maintaining core learning objectives.

### Workshop 1: "Inside the Machine"

**Focus:** Technical infrastructure and systems integration

**Description:**  
Students examine how multiple technologies (projection, audio, sensors) function as an integrated system. Using simplified demonstration equipment, groups investigate projection mapping geometry, sound localisation, and basic sensor response.

**Primary Adaptation:**  
Hands-on exploration of light and shadow, sound direction games, simple motion-reactive light using basic sensors.

**Secondary Adaptation:**  
Projection mapping demonstration using mini-projectors, spatial audio listening exercise, sensor calibration demonstration.

**Third-Level Adaptation:**  
Full technical demonstration including system diagrams, calibration data review, latency measurement discussion, Q&A with technical staff.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Understand how multiple technologies integrate into cohesive system
- Observe cause-effect relationships in technical infrastructure
- Appreciate precision required in installation and calibration

**Resources Provided:**  
Mini-projectors, directional speakers, basic sensors, system diagrams (secondary/third level).

---

### Workshop 2: "Sound in Space"

**Focus:** Spatial audio design and psychoacoustics

**Description:**  
Students explore how sound can be placed in three-dimensional space and how this affects emotional and physiological response. Workshop includes listening exercises, spatial audio demonstration, and creative sound placement activity.

**Primary Adaptation:**  
"Sound scavenger hunt" — locating hidden sound sources; discussion of how sound makes us feel; simple surround-sound demonstration.

**Secondary Adaptation:**  
Introduction to L-ISA concepts through demonstration; listening exercise with spatial audio examples; creative brief placing sounds in imagined space.

**Third-Level Adaptation:**  
Detailed examination of L-ISA system architecture; psychoacoustic principles (HRTF, localisation cues); professional workflow for spatial audio design; career pathways discussion.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Understand sound as spatial rather than merely directional
- Experience how audio placement affects emotional response
- Appreciate technical and artistic decisions in spatial audio design

**Resources Provided:**  
Directional speakers, L-ISA demonstration system (simplified for age group), listening examples.

---

### Workshop 3: "Make It Move"

**Focus:** Interactive and generative art principles

**Description:**  
Students create simple interactive artworks using accessible tools, exploring how input (movement, sound, touch) can generate output (light, sound, image). Workshop emphasises process over product and collaboration over individual creation.

**Primary Adaptation:**  
Shadow puppetry with projected light; sound-activated visual patterns using simple apps; group creation of "responsive wall" using basic sensors and lights.

**Secondary Adaptation:**  
Introduction to node-based visual programming (simplified); creating basic motion-reactive visuals; discussion of algorithmic/generative processes.

**Third-Level Adaptation:**  
Hands-on with actual tools used at Lightheart (TouchDesigner, Max/MSP, or equivalent); creating simple real-time system; examining code/logic behind responsive artworks.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Experience cause-effect relationships in interactive systems
- Understand input-process-output model in digital art
- Collaborate on creative technology project
- Appreciate complexity behind seemingly simple interactions

**Resources Provided:**  
Tablets/laptops with appropriate software (secondary/third level), simple sensors, projectors, facilitator guides.

## Booking Process

**Step 1: Initial Enquiry**  
Email education@lightheart.ie with:
- School/organisation name
- Proposed visit date(s) and preferred time
- Age group and approximate number of students
- Preferred workshop (if known)
- Any accessibility requirements
- Contact details

**Step 2: Availability Confirmation**  
Education team responds within 3 working days with:
- Availability confirmation (or alternative dates)
- Detailed programme outline
- Pricing confirmation
- Pre-visit pack (digital)

**Step 3: Booking Confirmation**  
School confirms booking by:
- Returning signed booking form
- Payment (or purchase order for invoice)
- Final numbers (7 days before visit)

**Step 4: Pre-Visit**  
School receives:
- Final visit details (arrival instructions, timing, contacts)
- Pre-visit classroom activities
- Risk assessment documentation
- Group leader briefing notes

**Step 5: Visit Day**  
Group arrives, facilitated programme delivered, feedback collected.

**Advance Booking Required:** 4 weeks minimum  
**Payment Terms:** Full payment or purchase order required 14 days before visit

## Accessibility

Lightheart is committed to accessible educational programming.

**Physical Access:**
- Step-free entrance and all gallery areas
- Accessible toilet facilities
- Accessible parking (limited; advise at booking)
- Seating available throughout gallery

**Sensory Considerations:**
- Exhibition includes periods of darkness and loud sound
- Quiet space available on request for students needing sensory breaks
- Pre-visit materials include sensory preparation guidance
- Facilitators trained to recognise and respond to sensory overwhelm

**Learning Support:**
- Facilitators adapt delivery for mixed-ability groups
- Visual timetables available for students who benefit from structure
- Additional adult helpers welcome (advise at booking)

**Communication:**
- ISL (Irish Sign Language) interpreter available on request (4 weeks notice)
- Materials available in large print on request
- Facilitators trained in clear communication techniques

**Risk Assessment:**
Full risk assessment provided at booking confirmation. Teachers/ group leaders retain duty of care for students throughout visit.

**Medical Requirements:**
Please advise of any medical conditions, allergies, or emergency requirements at booking. First aid trained staff on site.

## Teacher Resources

### Pre-Visit Pack (Provided at Booking Confirmation)

**Contents:**
1. **Programme Overview** — Detailed schedule, learning outcomes, curriculum links
2. **Exhibition Context** — Information about current exhibition (age-appropriate versions)
3. **Sensory Preparation** — Description of dark/light, loud/quiet moments; preparation strategies
4. **Vocabulary Guide** — Key terms with definitions (differentiated by level)
5. **Discussion Prompts** — Classroom conversation starters for before and after visit
6. **Risk Assessment** — Venue risk assessment and group management guidance
7. **Logistics** — Arrival instructions, contacts, timing, what to bring

### Post-Visit Activities (Provided at Departure)

**Suggested classroom follow-ups:**

**Primary:**
- Drawing/painting response to experience
- Creative writing: "The Room That Knew We Were There"
- Sound mapping exercise in school environment
- Group discussion: How was this different from other galleries?

**Secondary:**
- Critical review writing (exhibition review structure)
- Research project: Career in creative technology
- Debate: "Is digital art 'real' art?"
- Design challenge: Plan an immersive space for your school

**Third-Level:**
- Technical analysis paper examining infrastructure choices
- Comparative study: Lightheart vs. other immersive venues
- Curatorial proposal: Programme an exhibition for the space
- Reflection essay: Technology and presence in contemporary art

**Digital Resources:**
- Video clips from workshop demonstrations (shared post-visit)
- Extended reading list (age-appropriate)
- Career pathway information (secondary/third level)
