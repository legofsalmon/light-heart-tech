# Lightheart Annual Report — Year 1 Framework

**Document Type:** Planning framework for data collection and report structure  
**Coverage Period:** September 2026 — August 2027  
**Publication Target:** Late 2027  
**Status:** Planning / Data collection begins September 2026  
**Owner:** Operations Director (coordinated with Marketing, Finance, Artistic)

---

## Purpose

This document establishes what data to collect from Lightheart's first day of operation (September 18, 2026) to enable a comprehensive Year 1 Annual Report. It is not the report itself — it is the blueprint that ensures we capture the right information as we operate.

**Why this matters:** Retrospective data collection is incomplete and inaccurate. We must track metrics from day one.

---

## SECTION 1: LETTER FROM THE FOUNDER

### What Content Is Needed
A 600–800 word personal reflection from Brendan Dillon addressing:
- Opening day: what happened, what felt significant
- One thing that exceeded expectations
- One thing that surprised/challenged
- How the quarterly model performed in practice
- A specific visitor, artist, or moment that stayed with him
- What Year 2 needs to achieve

### Data to Collect from Day One
- Founder diary/journal entries (monthly prompts)
- Direct quotes from opening week interviews
- Specific visitor/artist interactions (names, dates, context)
- Major decisions made and rationale
- Photos of founder at key moments (opening, each exhibition launch)

### Example Copy (Tone Reference)

> "I stood at the door at 9:47am on September 18, watching the first visitors walk in. I'd imagined that moment for three years. What I didn't anticipate was how quiet they would become — not the hush of a traditional gallery, but something else. The L-ISA system had just cycled to the evening shore recording, and the room sounded like the actual sea. A woman stopped, closed her eyes, and stood still for two minutes. That was when I knew we'd built something different from what I'd planned — and better."

> "The quarterly changeover model sounded elegant on paper. In practice, it meant closing for three days every twelve weeks while we rebuilt the space from scratch. The first changeover (Digital Nature to Winter Light) took five days. The second took three. By the third, the crew had it down to thirty-six hours. This is what I mean when I say we're building systems, not just exhibitions."

## SECTION 3: EXHIBITION REVIEWS

### What Content Is Needed
One page per quarterly exhibition (four total) including:
- Exhibition title, dates, lead artist(s)
- 100-word curatorial statement
- Visitor quote
- Press quote
- 3–4 installation photos
- Key statistics (visitor numbers, member attendance, school visits)

### Data to Collect from Day One

**PER EXHIBITION:**
| Data Point | Collection Method | Owner |
|------------|-------------------|-------|
| Curatorial statement | Written at launch | Artistic Director |
| Visitor quotes | Exit surveys + social media monitoring | Marketing |
| Press quotes | Media coverage tracking | Marketing |
| Installation photos | Professional photography each exhibition | Marketing |
| Visitor numbers | Booking system export | Operations |
| Member attendance | Membership check-in data | Operations |
| School visits | Education booking records | Education |
| Artist payment/fee | Finance records (sensitively) | Finance |

### Example Copy (Tone Reference)

**DIGITAL NATURE**  
*September 18 — December 17, 2026*  
Artists: Cian Ó Broin, Sarah Chen, The O'Connell Collective

> "Seven artists were commissioned to create new work for our opening. We gave them the technical specifications — 37 projectors, 87 speakers — and asked what they would build if they could use all of it. The result was three distinct environments: Ó Broin's AI-generated shoreline, Chen's geometric forest, and the Collective's living data garden. 18,400 people visited. 94% of exit survey respondents said they would recommend it to a friend."

> "Digital Nature doesn't ask you to understand technology. It asks you to stand still and listen." — *Irish Times review, September 2026*

## SECTION 4: COMMUNITY IMPACT

### What Content Is Needed
3–4 pages covering:
- Education programme outcomes
- Artist development (commissioned artists, where they are now)
- Local partnerships (businesses, organisations, cultural institutions)
- Founding Circle community (what they built together)

### Data to Collect from Day One

**EDUCATION:**
- Pre/post visit surveys from schools (standardised questionnaire)
- Teacher feedback forms
- Student work created in response to visits
- Workshop attendance and outcomes
- Partnership agreements with schools/institutions

**ARTIST DEVELOPMENT:**
- Artist statements at commission and post-exhibition
- What each commissioned artist did next (shows, awards, opportunities)
- Artist quotes about the Lightheart commission process
- Technical skills gained (training provided)

**LOCAL PARTNERSHIPS:**
- Partnership agreements signed
- Joint events/programmes delivered
- Cross-promotional reach
- Economic impact (local supplier spend)

**FOUNDING CIRCLE:**
- Member event attendance
- Member feedback/survey results
- Community initiatives led by members
- Retention rate for Year 2

### Example Copy (Tone Reference)

> "We worked with 23 schools in Year 1, reaching 4,100 students. 78% of teachers said their students created work in response to their visit — not because we asked them to, but because they wanted to. One primary school in Finglas made a film about 'digital trees' that won a national competition. They brought the trophy to Lightheart on their second visit."

> "Of the 12 artists we commissioned in Year 1, 9 have since secured major exhibitions or awards. Sarah Chen's Digital Nature piece was acquired by the Irish Museum of Modern Art. Cian Ó Broin was invited to show at Ars Electronica. We didn't just present their work — we helped build their careers."

## SECTION 5: FINANCIAL SUMMARY

### What Content Is Needed
High-level financial overview (not full audited accounts):
- Revenue breakdown by category
- Major cost categories (staff, rent, programming, operations)
- Investment timeline (founder capital, ongoing commitment)
- Break-even projection
- Notes on financial strategy

### Data to Collect from Day One
- Monthly management accounts
- Revenue by category (automated from booking/POS systems)
- Cost tracking by department
- Cash flow statements
- Budget vs. actual variance analysis (monthly)

### Example Copy (Tone Reference)

> "Year 1 revenue was €487,000 against operating costs of €890,000. This deficit was planned and funded by founder investment. We projected a Year 1 loss as we established operations and built audience. Break-even is targeted for Year 3 at 65,000 annual visitors — a figure we believe is achievable based on Year 1's 47,000 with limited marketing spend."

> "Ticket revenue (62% of total) performed 23% above conservative projections. Membership revenue (21%) validated our community-funding model. Cafe revenue (11%) exceeded expectations, suggesting visitors are staying longer than anticipated."

## DESIGN NOTES (For Designer Brief)

- **Format:** A4 portrait, 24–32 pages
- **Tone:** Institutional but warm; confident but not boastful
- **Photography:** High-quality installation shots, candid visitor moments, behind-the-scenes process
- **Typography:** CRUNK brand system (Inter, JetBrains Mono)
- **Colour:** Full colour, with black and gold as dominant
- **Paper:** Consider recycled stock for print version; PDF version essential
