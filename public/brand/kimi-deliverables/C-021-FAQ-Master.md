## GENERAL (10 questions)

### What is Lightheart?
Lightheart is Ireland's first purpose-built immersive gallery. We present quarterly exhibitions of immersive art — experiences where 37 projectors and 87 speakers surround you with work by international and Irish artists. The 280-square-metre space on Dublin's quays is designed specifically for this form.

### Where is Lightheart?
Lightheart is located on the Dublin quays, adjacent to the Guinness Storehouse. The full address is [address TBD]. We're a 15-minute walk from Temple Bar, a 5-minute walk from the Guinness Storehouse, and served by multiple bus routes and the Luas [stop TBD].

### When does Lightheart open?
Lightheart opens to the public on 12 September 2026. Founding Circle members and press have preview access from 5 September. Our first exhibition, "Digital Nature" by Pauric Freeman, runs from 12 September to 6 December 2026.

### Who runs Lightheart?
Lightheart was founded by Brendan Dillon and is developed in technical partnership with IDIRNET (Kev Freeney, Creative Director; Krisjanis Berzins, Technical Director). The day-to-day operations team will be announced closer to opening.

### How is Lightheart funded?
Lightheart is a privately funded cultural institution. The €4–4.5 million infrastructure investment comes from founder and private sources. We do not currently receive government arts funding, which allows us to move quickly and take creative risks. We may apply for project-specific funding for individual exhibitions.

### Is Lightheart permanent?
Yes. Lightheart is permanent infrastructure, not a temporary exhibition or pop-up. The venue is built to operate for decades, with permanent technical installations (projectors, speakers, sensors) that are maintained and upgraded over time.

### How often do exhibitions change?
Exhibitions change quarterly — four times per year. Each exhibition runs for 12 weeks, then the space transforms completely for the next show. This means Lightheart is different every time you visit.

### Can I visit more than once with the same ticket?
No. Each ticket is valid for one 50-minute session. If you want to experience an exhibition multiple times, you need to book multiple tickets. Alternatively, join the Founding Circle for unlimited visits.

### Is there an age restriction?
Lightheart is designed for adults and young people aged 12 and over. Younger children may attend at parental discretion, but the 50-minute duration, darkness, and loud sound may not suit all children under 12.

### Is it suitable for children?
Children aged 12+ generally engage well with Lightheart exhibitions. Under-12s are admitted but parents should consider whether their child will be comfortable with 50 minutes of immersive sound and visual stimuli. See our relaxed sessions for a gentler experience suitable for younger visitors.

## VISITING (10 questions)

### How do I book?
Book online at lightheart.ie. Tickets are released in batches — sign up to our newsletter for advance notice. Walk-up tickets may be available if sessions are not sold out, but booking in advance is strongly recommended.

### How long is a visit?
Plan 60–90 minutes total. The immersive experience itself is 50 minutes. Arrive 15 minutes before your session for check-in. Allow 10–15 minutes after for the cafe, shop, or simply finding your feet again.

### Can I arrive late?
Late arrivals may not be admitted. If you miss your session, we cannot guarantee entry to a later slot. If you're running late, call us on [phone TBD] and we'll do our best to accommodate you, but we cannot promise entry.

### Can I leave and re-enter?
Once the 50-minute session begins, you cannot leave and re-enter without missing part of the experience. However, a quiet room is available if you need a break. Tell a staff member and they will guide you.

### Is there parking?
Limited parking is available nearby. We have two accessible parking spaces directly outside. For general parking, we recommend [locations TBD] or using public transport. The venue is well-served by bus and Luas.

### Is there a cloakroom?
Yes. We offer a free cloakroom for coats and bags. We recommend leaving bulky items as the immersive experience requires freedom of movement. Valuables remain your responsibility.

### Can I take photos?
Photography is generally discouraged as it distracts from the experience and can disturb other visitors. Some exhibitions may permit photography in designated areas — check signage or ask staff. Flash photography is never permitted.

### What should I wear?
Wear comfortable clothing and flat shoes. The experience involves 50 minutes of standing or slow walking. The floor is smooth but can be visually disorienting — flat, stable footwear is recommended.

### Is it wheelchair accessible?
Yes. Lightheart is fully wheelchair accessible with step-free access throughout. Both galleries have designated wheelchair spaces. Accessible parking and toilets are available. See our accessibility page for full details: lightheart.ie/accessibility

### Are guide dogs allowed?
Registered assistance dogs are welcome throughout Lightheart. Please let us know when booking if you'll be bringing an assistance dog so we can ensure appropriate facilities. Emotional support animals are not permitted.

## MEMBERSHIP (5 questions)

### What is the Founding Circle?
The Founding Circle is Lightheart's founding membership programme, limited to 200 members. Members receive unlimited exhibition access, priority booking, exclusive preview evenings, and permanent recognition on our founding wall. When 200 join, the Circle closes forever.

### How do I join?
Applications open in June 2026 at lightheart.ie/membership. Submit your application with your preferred tier (Circle €199, Inner Circle €499, Cornerstone €999). We review applications in order received. Not everyone is accepted — we look for genuine enthusiasm and cultural engagement.

### Can I gift a membership?
Cornerstone members (€999 tier) receive one gift membership to give away. For other tiers, you can purchase a membership as a gift by applying in the recipient's name. Gift memberships are not transferable after activation.

### Can I upgrade?
Yes. You can upgrade your tier at any time by paying the difference, subject to availability in your desired tier. If your preferred tier is sold out, you'll be placed on a priority waitlist for upgrades.

### What happens after year 1?
Founding Circle memberships are annual. If you maintain continuous membership, your founding price is locked permanently. If you allow membership to lapse and later rejoin, you pay the then-current rate (which will be higher). Your founding wall recognition remains permanent regardless.

## ARTISTS (5 questions)

### How do I propose a project?
Read our Artist Brief at lightheart.ie/artists. If your practice aligns with our approach, email artists@lightheart.ie with a one-page concept, your CV, and links to previous work. We review proposals quarterly and respond within 6 weeks.

### What are the selection criteria?
We evaluate proposals on artistic merit (40%), alignment with our curatorial principles (30%), technical feasibility (20%), and contribution to diversity (10%). Full criteria are in the Artist Brief. We prioritise work that could only exist at Lightheart.

### Do you pay artists?
Yes. We commission work and pay artist fees plus production budgets. Fees vary by project scope and are negotiated individually. We do not accept speculative work or "exposure" arrangements. Artists retain copyright; we retain documentation rights.

### How long is a commission?
The commission process typically takes 6–9 months from concept to opening. This includes technical consultation with IDIRNET, development, installation, and calibration. The exhibition then runs for 12 weeks.

### Can I use my own equipment?
Generally, no. Lightheart's infrastructure is permanent, calibrated, and integrated. Artists work with our technical systems rather than installing external equipment. Exceptions are possible for specific artistic requirements — discuss with our technical team during proposal stage.

## EDUCATION (5 questions)

### Can I bring my class?
Yes. We welcome school and group visits. Our education programme runs from October 2026 for primary, secondary, and third-level groups. Each visit includes a facilitated gallery experience and hands-on workshop.

### What ages?
Primary (8–12), Secondary (13–17), and Third Level (undergraduate/postgraduate). Each programme is adapted for age-appropriate learning. Children under 8 are not typically accommodated for school visits — consider our relaxed public sessions instead.

### How do I book?
Email education@lightheart.ie with your school name, proposed dates, age group, and approximate numbers. We respond within 3 working days. Bookings require minimum 4 weeks advance notice.

### What does it cost?
Group rates are [TBD]. One teacher per 10 students attends free. Additional adults pay standard group rate. Subsidised places are available for DEIS schools — ask when booking.

### What curriculum links?
Our programmes link to Visual Arts, Music, Computer Science, Physics, and SPHE. Specific learning outcomes are documented for each age group. Pre-visit and post-visit resources are provided. See our Education page for full details.

## PRESS (5 questions)

### Where do I get press images?
High-resolution images are available at lightheart.ie/press. For specific requirements or additional assets, email press@lightheart.ie with your deadline and usage details. Images are embargoed until agreed release dates.

### Can I interview the team?
Yes. Brendan Dillon (Founder) and Kev Freeney (Creative Director) are available for interview. In-person interviews at the venue are possible from August 2026; virtual interviews available now. Email press@lightheart.ie to arrange.

### Is there a press preview?
Yes. Each exhibition includes a press preview before public opening. Press are also welcome to visit during regular hours. Contact press@lightheart.ie to be added to the press list and receive preview invitations.

### Where is the press kit?
Download the full press kit at lightheart.ie/press-kit. It includes founder bios, technical specifications, image gallery, exhibition information, and key facts. Individual sections can be provided on request.

### Can I film inside?
Professional filming for broadcast or documentary purposes requires advance permission. Email press@lightheart.ie with your proposal, intended usage, and crew size. We accommodate most reasonable requests but require approval to protect the artwork and visitor experience.
